## Already Applied / In Pipeline — Query Supabase First

You have the Supabase MCP tool connected. Before doing any research, run these two queries to get the live state of my job pipeline. Use the results to build your exclusion list — do not hardcode anything.

```sql
-- Jobs already in my pipeline (applied, interviewing, rejected, etc.)
SELECT company, role, url, status
FROM public.jobs
ORDER BY company;

-- Jobs already in my research queue
SELECT company, role, url, decision, fit_score
FROM public.researched_jobs
ORDER BY fit_score DESC;
```

**Exclusion rules based on the query results:**
- Skip any URL already present in either table (exact match after stripping tracking params)
- Skip any `company + role` combination already present (case-insensitive, ignore punctuation)
- New roles at the same company are fine — e.g. if Cerebras has a Compiler Engineer in `jobs`, a different Cerebras new-grad SWE role is still worth surfacing
- Jobs with `status = 'rejected'` or `status = 'withdrawn'` in `public.jobs` — skip that specific role, but other roles at that company are fine
- Jobs with `decision = 'skipped'` or `decision = 'closed'` in `public.researched_jobs` — skip entirely

After querying, briefly summarize what you found (e.g. "You have 23 jobs in pipeline and 8 in the research queue — here are the companies/roles I'm excluding...") before starting the research.

## Important Research Instructions

1. **Visit career pages directly** — do not rely solely on Google snippets. Actually read the job descriptions.
2. **Check posting date** — if a posting appears to be from before January 2026, note that it may be stale.
3. **Verify the job is open** — if the page 404s or says "position filled," mark it closed and skip.
4. **For EDA companies**, look beyond "software engineer" titles — also search "methodology engineer", "CAD engineer", "physical design tools", "ML for EDA", "signoff engineer", "timing engineer".
5. **Do not invent jobs** — only include postings you can verify with a real URL.
6. **Depth over breadth** — it is better to thoroughly research 15 companies than to skim 40.
